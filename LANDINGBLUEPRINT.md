# Rune Landing Page Blueprint v3.0

*"Rune is where novels get finished."*

---

# Mission

The landing page exists to convince serious fiction writers that Rune gives them the best chance of finishing their novel.

Rune is not selling another text editor.

Rune is selling a manuscript workspace.

Every section should reinforce one belief:

> **Rune helps authors finish what they start.**

---

# Core Philosophy

Every section answers one question.

Every interaction teaches one lesson.

Every feature exists to help an author finish a manuscript.

Every scroll reveals one new idea.

Every design decision should increase clarity before beauty.

The visitor should never wonder:

> "What is Rune?"

Instead, they should quickly begin asking:

> "Why haven't I been writing my novel here?"

---

# Emotional Journey

The landing page should feel like reading the opening chapters of a novel.

Each screen should reveal a new idea.

Each scroll should move the visitor closer to believing Rune is the right place to write.

The journey is:

What is Rune?

↓

Why do I need it?

↓

How is it different?

↓

How does it help me finish?

↓

What's it like to write in?

↓

Can I trust it?

↓

How do I start?

---

# Screen 1 — Hero

## Question

"What is Rune?"

## Goal

Within five seconds the visitor should understand:

• Rune is software.

• Rune is built specifically for novelists.

• Rune is different from document editors.

## Emotional Goal

"This feels like it was built for me."

## Layout

The Hero fills exactly one viewport.

Left Side

* Rune logo
* Headline
* Supporting paragraph
* Primary CTA

Right Side

A calm, beautiful writing environment.

No sidebar.

No chapters.

No Arena.

No manuscript tree.

No product explanation.

Only a beautiful editor that invites the visitor to imagine writing.

Below the editor:

Theme pills

* Candlelight
* Obsidian
* London Fog

Changing themes is the Hero's only interaction.

Purpose:

To sell the feeling of writing.

Not the features.

The Hero introduces Rune.

It does not explain Rune.

---

# Screen 2 — The Problem

## Question

"Why isn't my current writing software enough?"

## Goal

Introduce the real problem.

Not Google Docs.

Not Word.

The blank-document mindset.

Novels eventually outgrow documents.

Writers lose momentum.

Drafts become messy.

Structure disappears.

The visitor should feel understood before Rune offers a solution.

---

# Screen 3 — The Manuscript Workspace

## Question

"So what makes Rune different?"

## Goal

Reveal Rune's biggest idea.

Rune organizes manuscripts, not documents.

This becomes the signature section of the website.

## Layout

Full viewport.

Large product showcase.

Interactive.

Three tabs:

Workspace

Focus Mode

Arena

---

### Workspace

Purpose:

Teach structure.

Animation:

The editor gently shrinks.

The manuscript unfolds.

Projects appear.

↓

Chapters appear.

↓

Pages appear.

↓

Progress appears.

Lesson:

"This isn't another document editor."

---

### Focus Mode

Purpose:

Teach calm.

Animation:

Everything unnecessary fades away.

Sidebar disappears.

Editor expands.

Whitespace increases.

The surrounding page subtly darkens.

For a few seconds the landing page itself becomes quiet.

The visitor should feel Focus Mode rather than simply seeing it.

Lesson:

"Nothing stands between me and my story."

---

### Arena

Purpose:

Teach momentum.

Animation:

The workspace transforms into Battle Mode.

Enemy.

Health.

Word counter.

Timer.

Arena is never presented as a game.

Arena is presented as a system for defeating hesitation.

Lesson:

"Rune helps me keep writing."

---

Every interaction teaches one lesson.

No interaction duplicates another.

---

# Screen 4 — Build Momentum

## Question

"How does Rune help me finish?"

Introduce:

* Arena philosophy
* Race Mode
* Goals
* Heatmaps
* XP
* Streaks

The message is not:

"We gamified writing."

The message is:

Motivation fades.

Momentum can be built.

---

# Screen 5 — The Writing Experience

## Question

"What's it like to write in Rune?"

This is where beauty becomes the focus.

Show:

* Themes
* Typography
* Focus
* Offline writing
* Instant saving
* Calm design

The purpose is emotional.

The visitor should want tomorrow's writing session to happen inside Rune.

---

# Screen 6 — Trust

## Question

"Can I trust my manuscript here?"

Communicate:

* Offline-first writing
* Automatic sync
* Reliable autosave
* Export ownership
* Privacy
* Testimonials (when available)

Trust should come from craftsmanship.

Not hype.

---

# Screen 7 — Pricing

## Question

"Can I start today?"

Simple.

Calm.

Honest.

The objective is beginning the manuscript.

Not maximizing subscriptions.

---

# Screen 8 — The Road Ahead

Replace "Coming Soon."

Celebrate Rune's direction.

Never apologize for unfinished work.

The visitor should leave believing Rune is actively becoming the best place to write novels.

---

# Final CTA

Finish with purpose.

Not features.

Not pricing.

The visitor should leave believing:

"I don't need another editor.

I need a workspace built to help me finish my novel."

---

# Universal Rules

Every screen occupies approximately one viewport.

Every scroll feels like turning a page in a novel.

Every interaction teaches one lesson.

Every feature answers:

"How does this help an author finish their manuscript?"

If something does not strengthen that story,

it does not belong on the landing page.

---

# Final Feeling

When the visitor closes the page they should not remember:

"A beautiful writing app."

They should remember:

> **Rune is where novels get finished.**
